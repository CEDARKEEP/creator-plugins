# Copywriting Frameworks & Power Words

Proven formulas for writing compelling titles, descriptions, and captions. These frameworks apply across all platforms and content types.

## Core Frameworks

### AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

Best for: longer descriptions, landing-style captions, educational content.

1. **Attention** — Grab with a bold, surprising, or pattern-interrupting element
2. **Interest** — Generate curiosity with a compelling detail or story
3. **Desire** — Show what's in it for the viewer (benefit, transformation)
4. **Action** — Imply or state what to do (click, watch, subscribe, save)

**Title example:** "This $5 Tool Replaced My Entire Kitchen (You Need This)"
**Description hook:** "I spent $500 on kitchen gadgets before discovering this one $5 tool does it all. Here's why 2M people switched..."

### PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

Best for: pain-point content, tutorials, how-tos, service-oriented content.

1. **Problem** — Name a pain point the audience has
2. **Agitate** — Intensify the discomfort (make them FEEL it)
3. **Solution** — Promise the answer

**Title example:** "Tired of Bloated Code? It Gets Worse. Here's the Fix."
**Description hook:** "Your codebase is growing 3x faster than your team can maintain. Every new feature adds tech debt. But there's a pattern that stops the spiral..."

### Hook-Value-CTA — Universal Framework

Best for: everything. The simplest, most versatile structure.

1. **Hook** (first 1-2 lines) — Grab attention in 1.5 seconds
2. **Value** (body) — Deliver actionable insight, story, or information
3. **CTA** (closing) — Natural, non-aggressive call to action

**Hook types:**
- Curiosity gap: "What nobody tells you about [topic]..."
- Relatability: "Struggling with [problem]? You're not alone."
- Shock/contrarian: "The biggest mistake I made with [topic]"
- Numbers: "3 simple steps to [result]"
- Direct benefit: "Here's how to [desired outcome] in [timeframe]"

**CTA types:**
- Question-based: "What's your experience with [topic]?"
- Community: "Join [X] others who..."
- Save/share: "Save this for when you need it"
- Soft: "Drop a comment if this helped"

### BAB — Before, After, Bridge

Best for: transformation content, case studies, product reveals.

1. **Before** — Describe the current painful state
2. **After** — Paint the desired outcome
3. **Bridge** — Show how to get there (your content/product/method)

**Title example:** "From 0 to 100K Subscribers: The Exact Strategy"
**Description hook:** "A year ago I had 47 subscribers and no idea what I was doing. Today I'm at 100K. Here's the exact framework that changed everything..."

### Curiosity Gap — Open Loop

Best for: titles, hooks, short-form captions. Creates a cognitive itch that demands resolution.

**Pattern:** Present partial information that the audience MUST complete.

**Examples:**
- "The one thing every chef does wrong" (what thing?)
- "I stopped doing THIS and everything changed" (what did they stop?)
- "Scientists can't explain why this works" (why does it work?)

**Rules:**
- The gap must be resolved by the content (don't bait-and-switch)
- Be specific enough to trigger curiosity, vague enough to require the click
- Never reveal the answer in the title/hook

## Psychological Triggers

### Power Words (boost CTR ~13%)

**Curiosity:** Secret, Shocking, Insider, Forbidden, Little-known, Hidden, Unexpected, Surprising, Strange, Mysterious

**Urgency:** Limited time, Hurry, Act now, Last chance, Only, Before it's gone, Don't miss, Now, Today, Deadline

**Emotion:** Unbelievable, Mind-blowing, Heartwarming, Terrifying, Incredible, Amazing, Insane, Epic, Brutal, Life-changing

**Authority:** Proven, Guaranteed, Science-backed, Expert, Official, Certified, Research-backed, Data-driven

**Value:** Free, Ultimate, Complete, Essential, Best, Top, Easy, Fast, Simple, Step-by-step

### Emotional Words (boost CTR ~7%)

Words that trigger an emotional response perform measurably better. Match the emotion to the content type:

- **Wonder:** Incredible, Stunning, Beautiful, Breathtaking
- **Fear/Concern:** Warning, Dangerous, Critical, Risky, Avoid
- **Excitement:** Epic, Insane, Game-changing, Revolutionary
- **Empathy:** Honest, Real, Raw, Vulnerable, Truth

### Negativity Bias

Negative framing often outperforms positive because people are more motivated to avoid loss than to seek gain:

- "Stop doing this with your money" > "Do this with your money"
- "7 mistakes killing your channel" > "7 tips to grow your channel"
- "Why your code is terrible" > "How to write better code"

Use judiciously — too much negativity feels toxic.

### Specificity Principle

Specific numbers and details increase credibility and CTR:

- "$437/day" > "make money online"
- "7 tips" > "some tips"
- "in 14 days" > "quickly"
- "2,847 people" > "thousands of people"

### Social Proof

References to popularity signal validated content:

- "1M people downloaded this"
- "Why 10 million people watched this in 24 hours"
- "The video YouTube doesn't want you to see"

## Bracket/Parenthetical Modifiers

Brackets in titles increase CTR by **38%** (HubSpot/Outbrain study). They serve as a "content sneak preview" and credibility boost.

**Effective bracket content:**

| Bracket | Signal |
|---------|--------|
| [2026] | Freshness, relevance |
| [FREE] | Value, accessibility |
| [PROVEN] | Authority, trust |
| [STEP-BY-STEP] | Clarity, structure |
| [WITH EXAMPLES] | Practical, actionable |
| [DATA] | Evidence-based |
| [CASE STUDY] | Real-world proof |
| [VIDEO] | Format indicator |
| [GUIDE] | Comprehensive resource |
| [NEW] | Novelty, freshness |

**Placement:** Typically at the end of the title.
**Format:** Square brackets `[GUIDE]` or parentheses `(2026)` — both work, brackets slightly outperform.

## Anti-Patterns — What NOT to Do

1. **Bait-and-switch:** Promising something the content doesn't deliver. YouTube actively penalizes this with demonetization and reduced reach.
2. **Over-promising:** "ULTIMATE GUIDE" for a 3-minute video. Superlatives the content can't support.
3. **Keyword stuffing:** Cramming keywords unnaturally. Harms readability AND rankings.
4. **Excessive jargon:** Alienates newcomers — often the largest potential audience.
5. **Being too vague:** "5 Great Hikes" — no specificity, no reason to click.
6. **Label headlines without verbs:** "Gardening Tips" has no energy. "Master Container Gardening in 7 Days" has action.
7. **Ignoring mobile truncation:** Burying the hook after 60+ characters.
8. **All caps everything:** Strategic ALL CAPS on 1-2 words is effective. ALL CAPS EVERYTHING IS SHOUTING.
